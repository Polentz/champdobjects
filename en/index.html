d'un champ à l'autre
/ von Feld zu Feld

FR DE EN

The project d'un champ à l'autre / von Feld zu Feld (from one field to the next) showcases a dialogue between art and local traditions that highlights the history and heritage of the Alpine valleys of Binn and Entremont in Valais. Designed by Collectif Facteur and least, in partnership with the Binntal Nature Park, this artistic and collaborative project invites everyone to take part in a creative adventure that celebrates the knowledge, stories and identity of these areas.

The first part of the project features a collective consultation, allowing participants to select an object to be exhibited at the Regional Museum Binntal as part of a new collection to be inaugurated in spring 2026. Which object do you like the most and why? By selecting an object, you take part in curating the future exhibition. This museum is yours too!

The project

d'un champ à l'autre / von Feld zu Feld (from one field to the next) is a co-creative project that invites the local community of Landschaftspark Binntal to forge links with objects from the collection of Mr. Gerd Graeser, held at the Binntal Valley Regional Museum. The aim is to actively engage citizens in a playful and captivating way in the selection of objects to be exhibited, enriching it with stories and meaning. Conceived by a transdisciplinary group, this project offers a unique opportunity to get involved in the future history of this collection, and to appropriate the museum as a living space. On the following pages, you'll find details of how you can get involved.

1. Postcards

Postcards are distributed via various channels to reach as wide an audience as possible. Participants choose an object from among those featured on the cards and write a story about it on the reverse. They then send the postcard, postage paid, back to the Binn Museum. The postcards are available from the Ernen and Binn tourist offices and the Binntal Regional Museum, among others. An online survey is also available via the project partners' websites (see below).

2. Photobooth

This stage brings the public face-to-face with objects from the collection. The objects are taken out of storage and photographed with the participants in a photo booth. Two photos are printed on the spot, one for the participant, the other for use in the exhibition at the Binn Museum and/or in the publication accompanying the project, with the participant's consent. The photo booth will be activated on several occasions.

Grengiols: FestPark, April 26, 2025
Ernen: Randonnées du Parc, May 31, 2025
Binn: Fête Dieu, June 22, 2025 at Giessersand

3. Cyanotype

In the third stage of the project, we're creating a large-scale cyanotype with the help of local schoolchildren and other interested parties. Cyanotype (Greek: impression in blue) is an ancient photographic process that uses UV light to make an impression of an object or negative on a photosensitive support. Participants place objects from their collection on a large cloth and arrange them as they wish. The fabric has previously been coated with a photosensitive product, and the sun's UV rays are used to print the shapes of the objects on the fabric. The result is a large image in shades of white and blue, which will be featured in the new exhibition and serve as a brand image for the show.

4. Publication and vernissage

To coincide with the opening of the collection's new exhibition, a publication outlining all stages of the project will be published. The d'un champ à l'autre / von Feld zu Feld project is part of a collaboration between the association least, the Collectif Facteur, the Regionalmuseum Binntal and the Landschaftspark Binntal.
